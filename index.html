The mechanism

The 5 Signals Your Training App Is Structurally Blind To

TrainerRoad, Zwift, Garmin — every algorithm coaches you on half the picture. Here's what they can't see, and what we read every week instead.

  • 1
    Real Sleep Quality Not hours in bed — actual deep-cycle recovery via Garmin or Oura. Most athletes at 47 have far less than they think. We read the number before we touch your plan.
  • 2
    Heart Rate Variability (HRV) HRV-guided training produced 7.3% more time-trial improvement than fixed plans in trained cyclists. Same hours. Same effort. One variable: whether the plan adapted to the rider. (IJSPP, 2019)
  • 3
    Life Load Work pressure, travel, family. At 47, your nervous system stacks cycling stress and life stress together. No algorithm accounts for that. We do.
  • 4
    Subjective Recovery How you actually feel when you swing a leg over the bike. No algorithm asks. Good human coaches do — and after a few weeks, they know when you're lying about feeling fine.
  • 5
    Fueling Status Chronic under-eating on ride days is the most under-discussed plateau driver in men over 40. You're starving the exact cellular repair processes that build watts.

Common questions

What Most Riders Ask Before Booking

I already use TrainerRoad. Why is it not working?

TrainerRoad has one of the best algorithms on the market — and it's structurally blind to the 5 signals that matter most for your body at 40+. It can only adjust based on what it can measure. Sleep quality, HRV, life load, subjective recovery, fueling — none of these feed its model. That's the entire premise of the 5-Signal Timing Model. The problem isn't your effort. It's your inputs.

I don't have time to add more training hours.

You won't. Every rider in the program has maintained their current volume or reduced it. The method is about making the hours you already ride produce the adaptation your body is ready for — not stacking more stress on a system that's already over-reached.

Will this work at my age? I'm in my early fifties.

The 5-Signal Timing Model was built specifically for men 40–60. Hormonal recovery, sleep architecture, life load — all of it is different at this stage. Every protocol accounts for that. Thomas was 52 and went from 275 to 301w in 10 weeks.

My schedule is unpredictable. I travel constantly.

Built into the system. When your signals show you're under-recovered from travel, we don't push intensity. The plan restructures around what your body can absorb that week — not what a static schedule says.

What if I fall behind or miss weeks?

The plan bends around your life. Nearly every rider who's gone through Second Wind came in with that fear. The ones who struggled weren't the ones who missed a week — they were the ones who didn't share their data. Your inconsistency isn't the problem. A plan that can't absorb it is.

How is this different from a random coach on TrainingPeaks?

Most coaches build solid plans. Very few read all 5 biological signals systematically every week and adjust training in real time around them. That specific method — not the plan design — is what's producing the gains we're seeing in men your age.

What time zones are covered?

PT, CT, and ET. Most of the work — plan adjustments, signal reviews, data reading — happens asynchronously. You're not locked into a fixed weekly call time. Everything fits around your schedule.
